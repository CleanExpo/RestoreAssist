## 1. Day-in-the-Life — Top 5 Moments Where Video Reduces Time-to-Competency

### Moment 1: Morning Dispatch (7:00 AM)
**What happens:** PM reviews scheduled jobs, assigns technicians, checks equipment inventory.\\
**Friction:** Technicians not certified for specific job types, equipment calibrations due, unclear job scope from overnight callouts.\\
**Video need:** "Morning Dispatch Dashboard" — 90-second screen recording showing how RestoreAssist auto-assigns jobs by certification level, flags equipment calibration status, and surfaces scope notes from the intake form.\\
**Impact:** Reduces dispatch time from 20 min to 5 min; prevents sending unqualified techs to Category 3 jobs.

### Moment 2: First Site Inspection (9:00 AM)
**What happens:** Technician arrives at wet property, needs to conduct thorough inspection, document everything, and leave with a report.\\
**Friction:** Techs forget S500 inspection protocol, miss evidence angles, take 45+ minutes when it should take 8.\\
**Video need:** "Full Inspection in 8 Minutes" — 3-minute POV video showing the complete inspection workflow: moisture mapping sequence, photo capture points (ceiling, walls, floor, affected contents), meter placement, contamination classification.\\
**Impact:** Reduces inspection time by 70%; standardises output quality across junior and senior techs.

### Moment 3: Report Writing (2:00 PM, back at vehicle/depot)
**What happens:** Technician or PM must draft the S500 report from field notes and photos.\\
**Friction:** Manual report writing takes 2–3 hours per job; inconsistent formatting; missed citations; reports submitted late (5–14 days), delaying payment.\\
**Video need:** "From Photos to Report in 30 Seconds" — 2-minute screen recording showing AI report generation: upload photos, review auto-populated S500 sections, edit rationale, sign off.\\
**Impact:** Reduces report writing from 2–3 hours to 15 minutes; same-day submission becomes the norm.

### Moment 4: Client Handoff (Evening)
**What happens:** PM or tech needs to present findings to customer, explain scope, and get sign-off.\\
**Friction:** Customers don't understand Category/Class terminology; they question photo evidence; scope disputes arise at handoff.\\
**Video need:** "How to Walk a Client Through Their Report" — 2-minute training video showing how to present the client portal, explain moisture readings visually, and use photo timestamps to build trust.\\
**Impact:** Reduces scope disputes by 60%; improves customer satisfaction scores.

### Moment 5: Insurance Adjuster Pushback (Days 3–14)
**What happens:** Adjuster queries scope, requests additional documentation, or disputes line items.\\
**Friction:** PM spends hours compiling evidence retroactively; photos weren't properly labelled; moisture logs are incomplete.\\
**Video need:** "Responding to Adjuster Queries in 5 Minutes" — 2-minute screen recording showing how to use RestoreAssist's evidence linking to pull up every photo, reading, and citation supporting a contested line item.\\
**Impact:** Reduces adjuster response time from hours to minutes; improves claim approval rate.

## 2. Pain Points — Service Company Perspective

### 2.1 Inspection Documentation
- Junior techs miss critical angles — no ceiling shots, no moisture meter-in-frame.
- No standardised protocol; every tech inspects differently.
- Photos saved to personal phones, not company system — lost when techs leave.
- **Severity:** High — incomplete documentation is the root cause of most downstream problems.

### 2.2 Report Writing (2–3 Hours Manual)
- PMs and senior techs spend evenings writing reports instead of growing the business.
- Inconsistent S500 formatting; outdated edition references.
- Reports submitted 5–14 days after job completion, delaying invoicing by weeks.
- **Severity:** High — directly impacts cash flow and technician utilisation.

### 2.3 IICRC Citation Format
- Techs don't know which S500 section governs which decision.
- Categorisation rationale is guesswork; no supporting evidence.
- Edition discipline is inconsistent across the team.
- **Severity:** Medium-High — citation errors are the primary reason assessors reject reports.

### 2.4 Client Handoff
- Customers don't understand technical terminology.
-_scope disputes arise because customers can't see the evidence.
- No professional presentation format; reports look like Word documents.
- **Severity:** Medium — slows approvals and creates avoidable rework.

### 2.5 Insurance Adjuster Pushback
- Adjusters request additional documentation that should have been captured.
- Retroactive evidence compilation takes hours per query.
- Disputed line items lack traceable evidence links.
- **Severity:** High — every day a claim is disputed is a day revenue is tied up.

### 2.6 Team Coordination
- No visibility into which tech is qualified for which job type.
- Equipment calibration status not tracked.
- Job status updates rely on WhatsApp or phone calls.
- **Severity:** Medium — operational friction that compounds across a growing team.

## 5. Script Outline — "How to Complete a Full Inspection in 8 Minutes"

**Format:** POV field recording + picture-in-picture screen recording
**Target:** Field technicians — both new hires needing certification prep and experienced techs wanting to speed up
**Length:** 3:30–4:00
**Tone:** Direct, operational, no fluff. Like a senior tech showing a junior how it's done.

---

### OPENING (0:00–0:15)

**Visual:** POV shot — technician parks van, grabs tablet, walks to front door. On-screen text: "8 minutes. That's the benchmark. Here's how."

**Voiceover:**
> "Your customer is stressed. Your insurance assessor wants detail. And you've got four more jobs today. Let's get this inspection right — and fast."

---

### SECTION 1: PRE-ARRIVAL SETUP (0:15–0:45)

**Visual:** Screen recording — technician opens RestoreAssist app, confirms job details, checks equipment calibration status.

**Voiceover:**
> "Before you walk in, confirm the job type and your certification level. Category 3 loss? You need your WRT and AMRT certs. Platform won't let you proceed if you're not qualified — that's protecting you and the company."

**Action:** Tap through job details. Green checkmarks on certification match.

> "Check your moisture meter calibration. If it's due, the app flags it. Don't guess — data that's out of calibration is evidence that won't hold up."

---

### SECTION 2: SITE WALKTHROUGH (0:45–2:00)

**Visual:** POV — tech enters property. App prompts for photos at each checkpoint.

**Voiceover:**
> "First photo: the front door. Establishes location and time. Every photo from here onwards is geotagged and hashed — your evidence chain starts now."

**Action:** Capture front door photo. App auto-stamps time and GPS.

> "Now the room sequence. The app prompts you through it: ceiling first, then walls, then floor, then affected contents. Don't skip steps — the app won't let you submit an incomplete sequence."

**Action:** Rapid sequence of ceiling photo, wall photo with moisture meter in frame, floor photo, contents photo.

> "Notice the moisture meter is always in frame. If the assessor can't see the reading, the photo doesn't count."

---

### SECTION 3: MOISTURE MAPPING (2:00–3:00)

**Visual:** POV — tech takes readings at multiple points. App builds a visual map in real-time.

**Voiceover:**
> "Take readings at every wall, every corner, every transition. Minimum six per room. The app builds your moisture map as you go — no sketching, no guessing."

**Action:** Tap reading points. Colour-coded moisture map populates on screen.

> "Red zone? That's above the salvageability threshold. The app auto-flags it and links to the S500 section that governs the decision. You don't need to memorise the standard — you need to document the evidence."

---

> "Class 4 loss — multiple rooms, structural materials affected. The app auto-calculates affected area from your photos and moisture readings. One less thing to manually calculate."

## Appendix: Messaging Guardrails

1. **Never position as replacing the technician.** The tool accelerates documentation; the technician makes the decisions.
2. **Never claim to eliminate fieldwork.** The app is for the van and the site — not the office.
3. **Always emphasise evidence quality over speed.** Speed is the outcome; evidence quality is the requirement.
4. **Use Australian English:** metre, litre, colour, programme (for structured activities).
5. **Field references:** Always show real equipment (moisture meters, thermal cameras) — no stylised illustrations.
6. **Tone:** Senior-to-junior respect. Not corporate training — practical mentoring.
7. **Phill McGurk appears only for strategic/compliance content.** He does not narrate field training videos.
