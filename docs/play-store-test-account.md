## Reviewer walk-through

The walk-through below covers all features that Play Console requires us to
demonstrate (camera capture, location, account deletion, subscription gate).

### 1. Sign in

1. Open the app (or `https://restoreassist.app/login` from a desktop browser).
2. Enter the credentials above.
3. The app lands on the dashboard.

### 2. Open the demo inspection

1. Tap **Inspections** in the bottom navigation.
2. Tap the row labelled `NIR-2026-05-PLAY1 — 12 Sample Street, Brisbane`.
3. Scroll down to see the photo grid, moisture readings, and **Sign
   Inspection** button.

### 3. Capture a photo (camera + location permissions)

1. From the inspection detail screen, tap the floating **Capture Photo** button.
2. Android will prompt for **camera** then **location** permission — accept both.
3. Take any photo (a wall, a desk — content doesn't matter for review).
4. The photo appears in the grid with GPS coordinates and a SHA-256 manifest
   under "Evidence integrity".

### 4. Sign-off flow

1. From the inspection detail, tap **Sign Inspection**.
2. The modal-first sign-off dialog opens on the unlocked form (because the
   Authorisation row is pre-seeded).
3. Sign on the canvas with a finger, tap **Confirm**.
4. The inspection status changes to `SUBMITTED`.

### 5. Subscription / billing surface

1. From the dashboard, tap **Settings → Subscription**.
2. The account is on a **TRIAL** plan with 30 credits remaining — reviewers
   can verify the upgrade-to-paid CTA is visible (they do not need to actually
   purchase).

### 6. Account deletion path (Play Store compliance check)

1. **Settings → Account → Delete account**.
2. The destructive confirmation dialog asks the reviewer to type
   `DELETE MY ACCOUNT`.
3. They do not need to confirm; the path is what Play Store verifies exists.
4. Public URL: `https://restoreassist.app/privacy#account-deletion`.

## Seed command

Operations regenerates this account end-to-end via:

```bash
DATABASE_URL=$DATABASE_URL_PROD npx tsx scripts/seed-playstore-test-account.ts
```

Behaviour:

- Idempotent — re-running upserts the user/org/inspection/photos/readings and
  rotates the password.
- Prints the new password to stdout on the final line. **Capture it and store
  in 1Password before closing the terminal.**
- Safe to run against staging (`DATABASE_URL_STAGING`) for smoke tests.
