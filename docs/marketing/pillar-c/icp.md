# RestoreAssist — Ideal Customer Profile

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Primary audience (BrandConfig):** restoration company owners and field technicians (AU)
**Secondary audience:** insurer claims teams and assessor networks

---

## Primary ICP: the owner-operator restoration business

### Firmographics

| Attribute | Range |
|---|---|
| Headcount | 1 (sole trader) → 5 technicians |
| Annual revenue | A$180k → A$1.2M |
| Years trading | 18 months → 12 years (skews toward 2–6 years — past the survival phase, hitting the admin wall) |
| Entity type | Sole Trader · Pty Ltd · Family Trust (mixed) |
| ABN status | Active, GST-registered. Pre-trading mode is supported but rare in the ICP. |
| Geography | All Australian states and territories. Volume skews NSW, QLD, VIC. |
| Service mix | Water damage (S500:2021) primary; mould remediation (S520:2024) common; fire/smoke (S700) opportunistic. |

### Roles in the buying group

- **Decision maker:** the owner-operator. Often holds the IICRC Water Damage Restoration Technician certificate themselves and runs payroll on a Sunday night.
- **Influencer:** the lead technician. Cares most about the photo + report flow on the van's iPad. Will quit the tool if the camera handoff is broken.
- **Blocker:** the bookkeeper or external accountant. Will reject anything that doesn't push clean line items into Xero, MYOB, or QuickBooks.

There is no procurement department. There is no IT department. The owner signs up, decides, and pays the same evening.

### Pains (ranked)

1. **Admin overhead is eating the night.** Two to three hours per evening typing job notes, building reports in Word, recalculating GST, and resizing site photos for insurer email attachments.
2. **IICRC compliance burden is real but invisible.** They know the report needs to cite S500:2021 §7.1 — they don't remember where in the PDF the section is, and they cite "industry standards" instead, which the insurer then queries.
3. **Insurer pushback on reports.** Missing photo timestamps, no GPS, ambiguous moisture readings, no clear scope-of-work. The insurer asks for clarification. The job stalls. Cash stops moving.
4. **The "AI tools" they have bought before were AI-flavoured, not AI-driven.** They paid a A$99/month subscription, watched a demo, and discovered the AI was a single "Generate summary" button that wrote two paragraphs of marketing copy.
5. **Re-keying line items into Xero.** They have a CRM, they have an accounting system, and they have a spreadsheet bridging them.
6. **No proof the platform actually works for them.** They had one tool last year that broke its Xero sync for three weeks before they noticed.

### Triggers (the moment they go looking)

- "Just lost a A$5,300 claim because the insurer rejected the photo evidence — no timestamps, no GPS, no continuous chain."
- "Bookkeeper quit. Need the CRM to feed Xero directly or I'm cooked."
- "Took on a second technician. The shared Google Drive folder system is now a fire."
- "RIA Australia conference last weekend — three other operators said they switched."
- "Insurer panel review next month. Need the reports tidied up before audit."
- "Got a quote from a US product. It charges in USD, doesn't know what ABN means, and the report template references the IRC."

### Vocabulary they actually use

- IICRC, S500:2021, S520:2024, S700, ANSI/IICRC
- Water Damage Restoration Technician (WRT), Applied Microbial Remediation Technician (AMRT), Applied Structural Drying (ASD)
- Class 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 water loss; Category 1 / 2 / 3 water
- Psychrometric chart, GPP (grains per pound), specific humidity
- AS/NZS 4849.1 (mould), AS 3666 (mechanical ventilation), state Building Code references
- "Scope of works", "make-safe", "non-restorable contents", "containment", "negative air"
- "Insurer panel", "assessor", "loss adjuster"
- Equipment slang: LGR (low-grain refrigerant), AFD (air filtration device), air mover

### Watering holes (where to be visible)

- **Restoration Industry Association of Australia (RIA Australia)** — member directory, newsletter, annual conference, regional meetups.
- **Facebook groups** — "Australian Water Damage Restoration", "IICRC Australia", "Restoration Industry Professionals AU". Heavy organic activity, allergic to anything that smells like an ad.
- **ServiceM8 and Ascora user communities** — Facebook + forum. Operators frustrated with the lack of restoration-specific report templates.
- **YouTube** — search behaviour is verb-led: "how to write a water damage report Australia", "IICRC S500 scope of works template", "Xero water damage invoicing".
- **Google Search long-tail** — "water damage report template Australia", "IICRC compliant inspection report", "ABN setup restoration business", "psychrometric chart calculator".
- **LinkedIn** — restoration owners do use LinkedIn, but mostly to follow insurers and IICRC instructors, not each other. The founder voice plays well here.
- **Trade nights** — Pickering's, Restorx, Steamatic, and equipment supplier (e.g. Phoenix, Dri-Eaz) trade events.

### Decision process

- Single decision-maker (the owner). Reaches out in the evening after a frustrating day.
- Wants to try, not to be sold. Books a demo only after they have already poked at a free trial.
- Low CAC tolerance: they will pay A$80–A$200/month per seat for a tool that proves itself in week one. They will *not* pay anything if onboarding takes more than 30 minutes before the first useful action.
- Sales cycle: 0 days (signs up on the spot) to 14 days (talks it over with their lead tech first).
- Cancels fast and cancels publicly if it doesn't work. Will post in the Facebook group.

### Channel preferences

- Email > LinkedIn DM > phone call. Will reply at 6am or 9pm, not 2pm.
- Hates webinars. Will watch a 60–120 second YouTube walk-through.
- Trusts other operators more than any vendor. Word of mouth is the dominant acquisition channel.

## Secondary ICP: insurer claims teams and assessor networks

Not the buyer in v1, but the silent influencer. They reject reports. They escalate to the operator. The operator then asks the CRM vendor why the report failed.

### Pains we relieve (indirectly)

- Inconsistent report formatting across operators on the panel.
- Missing IICRC citations or wrong edition references.
- Photos without timestamps, GPS, or continuity.
- GST and ABN errors on invoicing that block claim payment.

### Implication for messaging

We do not market to insurers in v1. But every operator-facing piece of copy that mentions the report quality is *also* read by their assessor. So claims like "every photo carries a C2PA-style manifest verified at read" and "IICRC citations carry edition and section" do double duty.

---

## Anti-ICP (do not chase)

- Restoration franchises with central IT (e.g. multi-state networks). Procurement loop is wrong for our motion.
- General builders who do occasional water damage. They want a general-purpose CRM, not a restoration-specific one.
- US-based operators. Compliance, GST, and report templates are wrong.
- Single-claim DIY homeowners. They are not the buyer.
