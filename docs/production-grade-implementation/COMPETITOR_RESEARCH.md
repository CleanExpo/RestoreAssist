# Competitor Research

Date: 2026-05-24  
Principle: Encircle-style field documentation is research inspiration only. RestoreAssist must not copy competitor UI, branding, colours, layouts, wording, or proprietary workflows.

## Strategic Takeaway

The market is converging on four buyer expectations:

1. Capture must happen in the field with very few taps.
2. Photos, rooms, sketches, comments, and scope must be connected by default.
3. Floorplans and measurements must be fast enough that technicians do not avoid them.
4. The office, adjuster, insurer, and customer need a clean handoff package without chasing the technician.

RestoreAssist can win by combining these expectations with AU/NZ restoration compliance, IICRC citation discipline, GST/ABN/state logic, insurer-specific requirements, and AI-guided evidence completion.

## Detailed Notes

### Encircle

Research signals:

- Encircle positions around field documentation, drying logs, floor plans, and Xactimate sketch import.
- Its value proposition is speed and defensibility of field documentation.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Do not copy Encircle's UI or wording.
- Build a distinct AU/NZ capture model: compliance status, claim readiness, IICRC citations, photo evidence, voice observations, and insurer handoff.
- The moat is not "we also do documentation." The moat is "we prevent missing documentation that causes AU/NZ claim friction."

### magicplan

Research signals:

- magicplan supports mobile floor plan creation and "Import and Draw" over an existing plan.
- Its restoration help content emphasizes documenting damage with photos tied to floorplan locations and using plan annotations for communication.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Fast floorplans are table stakes.
- A room graph should be the shared spine for photos, moisture readings, scope items, drying equipment, and report sections.
- The fastest v1 is not full 3D. It is import underlay, calibrate, trace rooms, auto-label, attach evidence.

### Matterport

Research signals:

- Matterport targets insurance/restoration with digital twins, 3D scans, remote review, and optional schematic/floorplan deliverables.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Do not force every claim into a 3D scan.
- Offer tiers: standard 2D room graph for most water losses, 360/LiDAR/large-loss package for disputed, commercial, or high-value claims.

### Xactimate

Research signals:

- Xactimate remains an estimating reference point. Sketch accuracy drives estimate accuracy.
- XactScope and guided estimating features emphasize estimate completeness.

RestoreAssist implications:

- RestoreAssist should not try to replace Xactimate immediately.
- Short-term: export evidence and quantities cleanly.
- Medium-term: build ESX/FML/estimate integrations or service partnerships.

### Dashpivot

Research signals:

- Dashpivot is strong in field forms, photos, workflows, and construction site documentation.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Template flexibility matters, but restoration users should not start with blank workflow builders.
- Default water/mould/fire/biohazard capture flows must be ready on day one.

### Buildxact

Research signals:

- Buildxact highlights takeoff, estimating, project management, Xero/QBO integrations, real-time pricing, and AI estimate review.

RestoreAssist implications:

- RestoreAssist needs estimate review, missing item detection, rate libraries, and Xero stability.
- Every estimate line should trace to room, evidence, material, quantity, and claim rationale.

### CompanyCam

Research signals:

- CompanyCam keeps photos organized by project, supports comments/descriptions, and produces structured photo reports.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Photo handling must become excellent, not incidental.
- Add automatic photo naming, location, room, stage, damage type, quality warnings, and photo report generation.

### DocuSketch

Research signals:

- DocuSketch claims quick 360 capture, timeline view, contextual comments, shareable documentation, ESX/FML sketches, and estimating services.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Timeline comparison is a strong pattern for restoration progress. RestoreAssist should implement a claim timeline: arrival, initial, demo, equipment, drying, final.
- Human estimator network is not a v1 requirement but could become an add-on once evidence packages are standardized.

### Simpro and ServiceM8

Research signals:

- Simpro and ServiceM8 own broad field-service operations: jobs, scheduling, invoices, payments, photos, forms, signatures, and mobile field work.
- ServiceM8 now advertises AI invoice generation, AI scheduling, and AI phone agent capabilities.

RestoreAssist implications:

- Do not build a generic field-service clone.
- Integrate with these systems where customers already use them.
- RestoreAssist should own the high-value restoration claim record and push/pull job, invoice, and customer data.

## Competitive Positioning

RestoreAssist should position as:

> The AU/NZ restoration compliance and claim-readiness layer for field capture, AI-assisted evidence, floorplans, reports, and insurer handoff.

It should avoid positioning as:

- Generic photo storage.
- Generic job management.
- Generic AI chatbot.
- Generic estimating software.
- A copy of North American restoration workflows.
