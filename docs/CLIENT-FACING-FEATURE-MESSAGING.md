## FAQ for Clients

### Q: What are regulatory citations?
A: They're official references to Australian building codes, electrical standards, and consumer protection laws that apply to restoration work. Basically, they're the "why" behind what you need to do‚Äîthe legal and regulatory basis for remediation recommendations.

### Q: Who decides which citations appear?
A: Our system automatically selects them based on your damage type, location, and the materials involved. If it's water damage in Queensland with electrical work needed, it'll pull relevant citations for those specific conditions.

### Q: Do I have to include them?
A: Nope. It's completely optional. We added it because some clients asked for more regulatory backing, but plenty of clients are happy with just IICRC standards. Your choice per report.

### Q: Do they cost extra?
A: No. It's included in RestoreAssist at no additional charge.

### Q: Will IICRC standards still be included?
A: Yes. IICRC S500, S520, and other standards are always included regardless of this option. Regulatory citations are just additional.

### Q: Can I change my mind after generating a report?
A: Absolutely. You can change the setting for each report and regenerate the PDF anytime.

### Q: Why would I want to include them?
A: A few reasons:
- Insurance claims often move faster with regulatory documentation
- It shows clients you're following all relevant Australian standards
- Professional appearance with authoritative references
- Makes it harder for insurers to dispute your recommendations

### Q: What if they're wrong or not applicable?
A: That's unlikely‚Äîour system is careful to only include citations that are actually relevant. But if you ever see something off, let us know. We're still refining this feature.

### Q: What reports can have regulatory citations?
A: Water damage, mould remediation, and fire damage reports. The system automatically selects standards relevant to the damage type.

### Q: Do I need to understand the regulations?
A: No. The citations are there to support your recommendations, not for you to interpret. Think of them as "proof" that what you're recommending is right.

### Q: Will my clients understand these references?
A: They don't need to. But they'll appreciate that you're following official standards. Insurers will definitely understand and appreciate the references.

### Q: What if the feature is disabled?
A: The toggle just won't appear. You'll get reports like you do now‚ÄîIICRC standards only. No change in functionality.

### Q: How do I know it won't break my PDFs?
A: We've tested extensively. The feature is completely optional and additive. If something goes wrong, the system falls back to IICRC-only reports automatically.
