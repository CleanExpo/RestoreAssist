EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Property owners who have experienced water damage, fire, mould, or
trauma events are in a state of heightened vulnerability. They are
overwhelmed by insurance processes, unfamiliar with restoration science,
and deeply anxious about whether their property will truly be restored.

This brief identifies 7 video topics designed to build understanding,
trust, and engagement with a restoration company that uses RestoreAssist
— a compliance platform that provides transparency, documentation, and
professional accountability at every stage of the restoration process.

SECTION 1: CUSTOMER PERSONA DEEP DIVE
======================================================================

WHO IS THE CUSTOMER?
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Primary: Residential property owner (homeowner or landlord)
- Secondary: Strata manager or commercial property owner
- Age range: 30-65 (predominant), with growing 25-30 first-home buyers
- Location: Australia-wide, concentrated in flood-prone and storm-prone
  regions (QLD, NSW, VIC coastal corridors)
- Insurance status: Majority hold building insurance; ~40% also hold
  contents insurance; some landlords have landlord protection policies

PSYCHOLOGICAL STATE AT POINT OF ENGAGEMENT:
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OVERWHELMED — Just dealt with burst pipe, flood, fire, or sewage event
2. VIOLATED — Strangers will be in their home for days/weeks
3. FINANCIALLY ANXIOUS — Uncertain about insurance coverage and excess
4. TIME-STARVED — Still working, managing family, dealing with insurer
5. INFORMATION-DEPRIVED — Doesn't understand restoration science
6. CONTROL-DEPRIVED — Feels powerless over their own home

KEY PAIN POINTS (What Customers DON'T Understand):
----------------------------------------------------------------------
P1. "Why is this taking so long?" — No understanding of drying science,
    moisture equilibrium, or why equipment runs 24/7 for days
P2. "Are they actually doing anything?" — Cannot see progress between
    technician visits; assumes fans running = no real work happening
P3. "Why do they need so many photos?" — Suspects documentation is for
    the contractor's benefit, not theirs (doesn't know it protects
    THEIR insurance claim)
P4. "How do I know it's actually dry?" — Doesn't understand moisture
    readings, baseline comparisons, or what "dry standard" means
P5. "Will mould come back?" — Deep fear of hidden damage resurfacing
    months later; no understanding of IICRC S520 mould remediation
P6. "Is the invoice correct?" — Cannot reconcile scope of work with
    charges; no transparency into line items
P7. "What happened while they were gone?" — No visibility into daily
    progress, readings, or decisions made on-site
P8. "Who is managing my claim?" — Confusion about the relationship
    between insurer, loss adjuster, builder, and restoration company
P9. "Why can't they just replace the carpet and be done?" — Doesn't
    understand subfloor drying, structural moisture, or secondary damage
P10. "How do I know these people are qualified?" — No visibility into
     certifications, standards compliance, or professional accountability

WHAT CREATES DISTRUST (The Trust Gap):
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Opacity: "I have no idea what's happening in my house"
- Jargon: Technicians speak in industry terms customers don't understand
- Inconsistency: Different techs say different things on different visits
- Surprise invoices: Charges that don't match verbal explanations
- No evidence: "They say it's dry — show me"
- Insurance friction: Restoration company and insurer seem misaligned
- Ghosting: Long gaps between communication when nothing is happening
- Previous bad experiences: Many customers have been burned before

WHAT WOULD BUILD TRUST (The Trust Formula):
----------------------------------------------------------------------
TRUST = Transparency + Evidence + Predictability + Accessibility

- SEE the process: Real-time visibility into what's happening
- UNDERSTAND the why: Plain-language explanations at each stage
- TRACK progress: Visual proof that things are moving forward
- CONTROL access: Know who is coming, when, and what they'll do
- VERIFY claims: Independent evidence (photos, readings, standards)
- PREDICT outcomes: Clear timelines with realistic expectations

VIDEO 1: "What Happens After the Water Stops"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE:        Process demystification / expectation setting
TARGET EMOTION: Relief — "Finally, someone explains this clearly"

EMOTIONAL HOOK:
"Your pipe burst is fixed. But the real work — the work that protects
your home and your health — is just beginning. Here's exactly what

"putting fans on it." Understanding the stages helps you know what to
expect, what good restoration looks like, and how to hold your
restoration company accountable. Every step is documented, measured,
and verified.

- 2:15-2:45  — Stage 5: Clearance testing & restoration sign-off
- 2:45-3:00  — CTA: "Your restoration company should show you all of

TONE: Calm, authoritative, reassuring
