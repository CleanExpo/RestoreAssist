# Competitive Intelligence: AU/NZ Insurer & Restoration Software Market

**Date:** April 2026
**Prepared for:** RestoreAssist (RestoreAssist.com.au)
**Classification:** Internal Strategic Document

---

## Executive Summary

- **Claims Central Property** (Claim Central Pty Limited) entered voluntary administration in October 2025 with Olvera Advisors appointed as administrator. The collapse was triggered by the loss of over $50M of insurer-contracted work from Hollard and Commonwealth Insurance. The technology arm — **Wilbur** — was pre-emptively separated as a standalone business by founder Brian Siemsen in early 2025 and survived intact.
- **Brian Siemsen** continues to operate **Wilbur** (wilbur.io) as a standalone SaaS claims management platform serving insurers, MGAs, TPAs and brokers in AU, NZ, US, and South Africa. Wilbur was recognised as a Celent "Technology Standout" in 2024 and launched AI/analytics tooling in September 2024. Wilbur is the most direct AU/NZ software competitor to RestoreAssist on the insurer-facing side.
- **The AU/NZ insurer software market** is dominated by Guidewire (core claims — IAG, Suncorp, QBE) and Duck Creek (Suncorp, RAA), with field/supply-chain layers handled by **Codafication Crunchwork** (IAG, Suncorp, QBE, Youi, Auto & General) and **CoreLogic/Cotality's Prime Ecosystem/Contractor Workspace** (acquired January 2025). Verisk/Xactimate operates via distributor Insurtech Systems (subsidiary of Stream Group ASX). Symbility (now CoreLogic Claims Workspace) is deployed at Vero NZ.
- **Verisk** is a $~14B market cap insurance data and analytics company with $2.88B in 2024 revenue. Its attempted $2.35B acquisition of roofing contractor software AccuLynx was blocked by FTC antitrust review (terminated December 2025) — a major strategic setback that signals Verisk's appetite for contractor-facing field services software. The FTC blockage creates an opening for non-US targets.
- **RestoreAssist's most credible expansion path** is positioning as the **IICRC-compliant restoration operations platform** for the AU/NZ market — sitting downstream of Guidewire/Duck Creek (insurer core) and upstream of accounting systems. The gap left by Claim Central's collapse and the absence of a local, IICRC-native field app creates an immediate displacement opportunity.

## 1. Claims Central Collapse

### Background

**Claim Central Consolidated (CCC)** was founded in Sydney in 2000 by Brian Siemsen as a full-service insurance claims management and technology company. At its peak it operated across property, motor, cyber and SME insurance claims in Australia, NZ, the US and South Africa. Its core products were:

- **Wilbur** — a modular SaaS claims management platform (evolved from the internal "ClaimLogik" system)
- **LiveGenic** — real-time video/photo claims tool
- **Hello Claims** — motor vehicle assessment and repair service

**AUB Group** invested in Claim Central Group as a strategic shareholder, with board representation, and later acquired the **Hello Claims** motor assessing brand in **July 2024**.

### Collapse Timeline

| Date                    | Event                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Early 2025              | Wilbur Technologies separated as standalone business under Brian Siemsen, independently capitalised                                     |
| July 2024               | AUB Group acquires Hello Claims motor brand from Claim Central                                                                          |
| **17 October 2025**     | Claim Central Pty Limited enters **voluntary administration**; Olvera Advisors appointed as administrator (ASIC Notice ACN 102 685 343) |
| 20 October 2025         | ASIC Published Notice issued (NSW)                                                                                                      |
| 21 October 2025         | Claim Central NSW Pty Ltd (ACN 657 554 624) also enters voluntary administration                                                        |
| 22 October 2025         | InsuranceNews.com.au reports the administration; CGU begins contacting affected customers                                               |
| 27 October 2025         | CGU publicly confirms it is contacting customers affected by the Claim Central halt                                                     |
| 14 November 2025        | ASIC meeting notice published — creditors meeting under s.439A                                                                          |
| 24 November 2025        | Creditors approve deed proposal; **unsecured creditors expected to receive 2.74c–6.32c in the dollar**                                  |
| November 2025 (pending) | Deed cannot proceed without CBA's consent (secured creditor, owed ~$1.5M)                                                               |

### Causes of Failure

Per industry reporting (InsuranceNews.com.au):

1. **Loss of major insurer contracts** — Over **$50 million** of contracted restoration work from **Hollard** and **Commonwealth Insurance** was withdrawn as those insurers consolidated or internalised their repair panels
2. **Benign catastrophe period** — Reduced claims volumes due to lower natural disaster frequency compressed revenue
3. **Margin squeeze** — Rising labour and subcontractor costs locked into fixed-price or capped-rate insurance contract structures
4. **Supply chain strain** — Post-COVID trade shortages
5. **Failed sale talks** — Business entered administration after failing to find a buyer

### Software Assets Outcome

- **Wilbur** was separated and independently capitalised before the administration — it survived and continues to operate
- **Hello Claims** (motor) was already sold to AUB Group (July 2024) — survived
- **LiveGenic** video tool — incorporated into Wilbur's platform, operating
- The **property restoration / claims management services business** (the field operations side) is what collapsed

## 2. Brian Siemsen's Current Business

### Wilbur (wilbur.io)

Brian Siemsen is the **Founder and CEO of Wilbur**, a standalone insurtech SaaS company headquartered in The Rocks, Sydney NSW. He has 28 years of experience in the insurance technology sector.

Wilbur describes itself as "Claims Management. By Design." — a modular, AI-powered SaaS platform for insurance claims management.

### Platform Capabilities

| Module                              | Description                                                                                  |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Claims Workflow                     | Configurable first-notification-of-loss to final resolution, custom workflows per claim type |
| Supplier Network                    | Connects insurers with repair/assessment supplier networks                                   |
| LiveGenic Integration               | Real-time video/photo capture paired with AI analysis for faster assessment                  |
| BAIS Policy Integration             | Partnership with BAIS for policy + claims combined solutions                                 |
| AI & Analytics (launched Sept 2024) | Fraud detection, underwriting analytics, workflow automation                                 |

### Markets Served

- **Australia** — primary market; insurers, MGAs, brokers, TPAs
- **New Zealand**
- **South Africa**
- **USA** — via exclusive licensing with Eberl Claims Service

### Clients

Wilbur does not publicly list all clients, but available information indicates it serves:

- General and specialty P&C insurers (property, cyber, automotive, commercial)
- MGAs and TPAs

### Industry Recognition

- **Celent "Technology Standout"** — 2024 Claims Systems Vendors report
- Special recognition for user interface organisation and ease of understanding
- Affordable pricing noted relative to enterprise competitors (Capterra AU reviews)

### Strategic Position Post-Claim Central

Wilbur is now an independent technology company, separated from the collapsed field services operations. This means:

- No operational liability from Claim Central Property's administration
- Technology IP fully preserved and operational
- Brian Siemsen retains full executive control
- The company's market position is potentially strengthened by the field services collapse (technology reputation unaffected)

**Key observation for RestoreAssist:** Wilbur operates at the insurer/TPA layer (claims workflow platform) — it does not provide field-side IICRC compliance documentation, moisture mapping, scope generation, or water damage-specific reporting. There is limited direct overlap with RestoreAssist's current product surface, but Wilbur is the likely competitor if RestoreAssist builds upward into insurer-facing claims integration.

### Key Standards Context

The **AS-IICRC S500:2025** standard was published by Standards Australia in early 2025 — Australia's first nationally recognised standard for water damage restoration. This creates a regulatory tailwind: insurers (Suncorp welcomed the standard publicly via Greg Buchhorn, Executive Manager Claims Procurement) and contractors are now required to document compliance. No existing insurer-side platform natively supports AS-IICRC S500:2025 documentation at the field level.

### AU/NZ Presence

Verisk operates in AU/NZ through **Insurtech Systems Pty Ltd**, a subsidiary of ASX-listed **Stream Group Ltd**, which holds the exclusive distribution rights for Xactimate products in Australia and New Zealand.

- Xactimate provides AU/NZ-localised pricing databases
- Products available: Xactimate, mobile claims, contents replacement, property maintenance
- AU market penetration is lower than US — Xactimate is known by industry but not as widely mandated by AU insurers as it is by US carriers
- Verisk hosted no Sydney-specific events in 2024–2025 (contrasted with Guidewire's Sydney forum in May 2025)

### AccuLynx Acquisition Attempt — Critical Strategic Signal

In **July 2025**, Verisk announced a $2.35B cash acquisition of **AccuLynx**, a SaaS platform for residential property contractors (primarily roofing) focused on insurance-driven restoration work. AccuLynx offered: CRM, lead generation, measurements, materials ordering, payment processing, job management.

The FTC requested additional information in October 2025, signalling antitrust scrutiny. On **December 29, 2025**, Verisk terminated the agreement after the FTC failed to complete review by the deadline. AccuLynx disputes the termination.

**Strategic implication:** Verisk was willing to pay $2.35B for a contractor-facing restoration workflow platform. The FTC blocked a US deal on market concentration grounds. Verisk's stated rationale was to "augment network capabilities across the insurance claims and restoration ecosystem." This signals:

1. Verisk recognises the strategic value of contractor-side field software
2. A non-US acquisition would face fewer antitrust barriers
3. An AU/NZ-native, IICRC-compliant restoration platform would be an attractive international expansion vehicle

## 5. RestoreAssist Market Expansion Path

### Current Position (April 2026)

RestoreAssist is an AU-native, IICRC S500:2025 compliant platform serving water damage restoration professionals. Core capabilities include:

- Report generation (Tier 1/2/3 question flows)
- Moisture mapping and drying logs
- Scope of works generation (AI-assisted)
- Photo documentation
- Inspection management
- Integrations: Ascora, Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks, Guidewire (field-side)
- Authority forms and e-signatures
- Client portal

### Strategic Expansion Path

#### Phase 1: Dominate Field-Side Restoration (Current → 12 months)

**Opportunity:** Claim Central's collapse left a gap in the AU restoration market — the primary managed-services layer between insurers and contractors is gone. Restoration contractors who relied on Claim Central's workflow are now underserved.

**Competitors to displace at this layer:**

- Codafication Crunchwork (strong at insurer layer, weak at individual contractor IICRC documentation)
- Xcelerate / Encircle Hydro (US-built, not AS-IICRC S500:2025 native, no AU pricing data)
- Manual processes / Excel (majority of AU restorers)

**Key capabilities to build/strengthen:**

- AS-IICRC S500:2025 compliance certification badges and audit trails
- RIA (Restoration Industry Association) membership integration
- Offline-capable mobile app (Capacitor) for on-site use

#### Phase 2: Insurer-Facing Platform (12–24 months)

**Opportunity:** Insurers (IAG, Suncorp, QBE) use Guidewire as core claims but need a field-side layer for restoration supply chain management. Codafication has the supply chain orchestration layer (Crunchwork) but lacks the **IICRC compliance documentation** and **restoration-specific data** (moisture readings, drying goals, equipment logs).

**Competitors to displace:**

- Codafication — no IICRC documentation; no scope generation; no moisture mapping
- Wilbur — claims workflow platform; no field restoration compliance tools
- CoreLogic Contractor Workspace (formerly Prime Ecosystem) — contractor CRM/job management; not IICRC-specific

**Key capabilities needed:**

- Bidirectional Guidewire ClaimCenter API integration (receive claim, return scope + documentation package)
- Insurer-facing dashboard (claim status, compliance scores, photo evidence)
- Panel management module (insurer assigns RestoreAssist contractors to claims)
- SLA tracking aligned to AS-IICRC S500:2025 drying standards

#### Phase 3: Ecosystem Play / Acquisition Target (24–48 months)

**The moat:** A platform that sits at the intersection of:

- **Field compliance data** (IICRC, AS-IICRC S500:2025 documentation)
- **Insurer connectivity** (Guidewire, Duck Creek API)
- **Pricing intelligence** (AU-specific restoration cost data, Xactimate-compatible)
- **Contractor network** (panel management, certification tracking)

**Acquisition candidates:**

| Acquirer               | Strategic Rationale                                                                                                                | Likelihood                              |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Verisk**             | Failed AccuLynx deal; needs AU/NZ entry; IICRC compliance data + Xactimate integration = direct synergy                            | High — if Xactimate integration is live |
| **CoreLogic/Cotality** | Already acquired Prime Ecosystem (AU contractor software Jan 2025); IICRC + estimating would complete their AU restoration stack   | Medium-High                             |
| **Guidewire**          | Marketplace ecosystem strategy; 110+ cloud integrations; AU-native Guidewire app with IICRC compliance is a Marketplace product    | Medium                                  |
| **IAG / Suncorp**      | Insurer strategic investment (cf. AUB + Claim Central); vertical integration of claims supply chain                                | Medium                                  |
| **Codafication**       | Merger to combine supply chain orchestration (Crunchwork) with IICRC field compliance — creates end-to-end AU restoration platform | Low-Medium                              |

### Competitive Displacement Summary

| Competitor                         | Weakness RestoreAssist Can Exploit                                                      | Displacement Timeline                              |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Claim Central (collapsed)**      | Customer base now unserved; 50+ insurer-contracted restoration businesses need platform | Immediate (now)                                    |
| **Wilbur**                         | No field restoration tools; no IICRC compliance; no scope generation                    | 12–18 months (insurer integration needed first)    |
| **Codafication Crunchwork**        | Insurer-facing only; no IICRC documentation for field teams; no moisture mapping        | 18–24 months (requires insurer API layer)          |
| **CoreLogic Contractor Workspace** | Generic contractor CRM; not IICRC-native; no AS-IICRC S500:2025 compliance              | 12 months (at field/contractor layer)              |
| **Xactimate AU**                   | Estimating-only; no IICRC field compliance; no drying logs; distributed via third party | Complementary integration better than displacement |
| **Encircle Hydro**                 | US-built; not AS-IICRC S500:2025 native; no AU pricing; no insurer integrations         | 6–12 months (contractor acquisition)               |

### Key Risk: Codafication

Codafication is the most credible competitive threat to RestoreAssist's Phase 2 (insurer-facing) ambitions:

- Already contracted with all major AU insurers (IAG, Suncorp, QBE, Youi, RAA, Auto & General)
- Won Guidewire's 2024 Insurtech Vanguards competition
- Backed by IAG as a reference customer
- Expanding internationally (North America, UK, Europe)

However, Codafication is a **supply chain orchestration platform** — it connects insurers to contractor networks. It does not provide field-level IICRC compliance documentation, moisture mapping, psychrometric calculations, or water damage classification. RestoreAssist should position as **Codafication-compatible** (a Crunchwork supplier integration) rather than as a direct competitor, while building the insurer API capabilities independently.
